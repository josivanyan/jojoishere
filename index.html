Achievements

Summary

Xiamen Marathon

Xiamen, a beautiful coastal city, is my favorite. In 2014, I completed the Xiamen marathon for the first time in 3 hours and 42 minutes, and then again in 2015 and 2017.

The marathon course takes you across a bridge suspended above the sea, creating a sensation of running on the water.

Shanghai Marathon

I worked in Shanghai for six years, and although it was very difficult to secure a spot in the race, I was fortunate enough to participate in the event in 2017.

The entire racecourse was interwoven between the bustling financial center of China and the alleys of traditional Chinese culture, making for a truly unique and unforgettable experience.

HongKong Marathon

The Hong Kong Marathon boasts one of the most arduous racecourses in Asia, featuring an interminable elevated bridge that leads into a sweltering tunnel.

Gazing at the endless ascent in the final 5 miles can evoke a sense of despair, yet the sense of fulfillment upon crossing the finish line is unparalleled by any other race.

New York Marathon

The New York Marathon is one of the six major marathons I have participated in, and I was fortunate enough to qualify for the race in 2018.

This event allows you to fully experience the diversity and enthusiasm of this great city.

The route from Staten Island to Central Park is undoubtedly the best way to get to know New York.

Berlin Marathon

The 2019 Berlin Marathon was held under inclement weather, with a cold and rainy day posing an additional challenge.

Nevertheless, the world-renowned racecourse proved to be an immense source of inspiration and invigoration for all participants.

The legendary athlete Bekele emerged as the winner, with a result that fell just short of the world record by less than a minute.
